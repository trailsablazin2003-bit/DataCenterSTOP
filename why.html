Understanding the impact on Marshfield

Massive Energy Consumption
Data centers require enormous amounts of electricity to operate servers 24/7. In a small community like Marshfield, this increased demand can strain local infrastructure and drive up utility costs for residents.
Water Usage and Resource Strain
Cooling systems for large data centers use significant amounts of water. In a smaller town, this can reduce available resources and place pressure on local water systems, leading to increased monthly water bills.
Lower Property Values
Large industrial-style facilities can make nearby neighborhoods less desirable, potentially lowering home values and affecting long-term community investment.
Environmental Impact
Energy production and backup generators can contribute to air pollution. In a smaller community, these effects are felt more directly and can impact overall quality of life.
Noise Pollution
Constant operation of cooling systems and generators can create ongoing noise. This disrupts the quiet environment that residents expect in a small town like Marshfield.
